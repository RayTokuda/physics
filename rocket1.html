Deriving the Rocket Equation

We start with the Impulse-Momentum Theorem:

F Δt = m Δv

Force applied over time equals change in momentum.

For rocket exhaust, the "velocity change" is the exhaust velocity (ve) and the "mass" is the mass of the ejected fuel (Δm).

F Δt = Δm · ve

Divide both sides by the time interval (Δt) to solve for Thrust (F):

F = ve · (Δm / Δt)

Thrust = (Exhaust Velocity) × (Mass Flow Rate)
