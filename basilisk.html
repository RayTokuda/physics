The Basilisk Lizard in action

Basilisk lizards can run across the top of a water surface. With each step, a lizard first slaps its foot against the water and then pushes it down into the water rapidly enough to form an air cavity around the top of the foot.

To avoid having to pull the foot back up against water drag, the lizard withdraws the foot before water can flow into the air cavity. During this full action of slap, downward push, and withdrawal, the average upward impulse on the lizard must match the downward impulse due to gravity if the lizard is not to sink.
